\chapter{Vectors \& Spaces}
\section{Vectors in 3D: Equations of Lines and Planes}
The closest distance between two lines is measured by the line which joins the two lines, and is in a direction which is perpendicular to both. This is why the cross product appears in the formula as follows in \cref{eq:distance_two_lines}.

\begin{equation}
    \label{eq:distance_two_lines}
    d = \norm{(\vec{r}_{line 1} - \vec{r}_{line 2})\cdot \hat{m}}, \quad \hat{m} = \frac{\vec{v_1} \times \vec{v_2}}{\norm{\vec{v_1} \times \vec{v_2}}}
\end{equation}

This gives the projection of the distance between any two points on both lines, in the direction of $\hat{m}$, which is the closest distance between the two lines.
